About Me

My name is Nicole Apsche, and I live in West Philadelphia. I moved to Philadelphia in 2012 to attend Temple Univeristy, where I earned my Bachelor's Degree in Neuroscience in 2016. I found neuroscience really interesting, but I loved my bartending job more. I had worked in restaurants growing up, and worked in them all through college. I fell in love with working as a team with my coworkers, and learned many valuable life skills, like organization, customer service, and how to make a perfect margarita. I also got to meet people from all walks of life, which I loved. I worked really hard and was able to pay off my student loans. I loved my time at Copabanana, but it was time to expand my horizons and find a sustainable career.

I began working as a full cycle recruiter for KGTiger, a talent acquisition company based out of Bala Cynwyd, PA. Shortly after, I was promoted to Account Executive, and tasked with the responsibility of managing several different accounts for the company, as well as a team of recruiters. I really enjoyed getting to work in a wide range of industries, as well as using the technologies I was learning to help others. I soaked up a lot of leadership idealogies from the President, Rasagna Holt. She taught me a lot about business, and also sparked my interest in coding. I enrolled into the Full Stack Certification Course at the University of Pennsylvania. Unfortunately, I was recently displaced due to COVID, but it gave me an opportunity to really focus and pour all of my energy into learning how to code. Before starting this course, I did not even own a computer. I am really proud of myself for how far I have come in the past 24 weeks. I really believe that my teamwork and leadership skills will be an asset to any team. I am excited to continue learning more programming languages and to begin my career as a developer.

In my spare time, you can find me running at the art museum, snuggled up watching movies, traveling, and spending time with friends. In the future, I would like to read some of the books that I have collected through the years. I look forward to finding a position where I can utilize my skills and really contribute to a team. I look forward to growing my skillset, and would like to find a company where I can stay and grow with.
